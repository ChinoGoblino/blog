A Short Note on Focus

2026-02-01
Writing

Uninterrupted time is not the same as focused time. You can sit alone for two hours and produce nothing worth keeping. You can have twenty minutes between meetings and write something you’re proud of.

The variable that matters is not the absence of interruption but the presence of a single clear problem. If you know exactly what you’re trying to do when you sit down, the time compresses. If you don’t, no amount of silence will help.

This is harder to arrange than it sounds. Picking the right problem to work on is its own kind of work — often harder than the work itself.
